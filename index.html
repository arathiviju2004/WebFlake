HISTORY

Christmas, with its origins in the Christian celebration of Jesus Christ's birth, has evolved into a globally celebrated holiday that weaves together religious, cultural, and secular influences. Initially tied to the Roman festival of Saturnalia and the winter solstice, Christmas faced historical opposition but experienced a Victorian-era resurgence, shaping the holiday's modern character with traditions like the Christmas tree and Dickensian themes of generosity. The international popularity of Santa Claus and the widespread adoption of gift-giving further underscore the holiday's fusion of diverse customs. Today, Christmas transcends its religious roots, representing a time for unity, generosity, and global festivities, where cultures blend and adapt, creating a tapestry of traditions that reflect the ever-changing nature of this cherished celebration.

FACTS

Origin of Christmas Tree

The tradition of decorating Christmas trees originated in 16th-century Germany. It became widely popular in the 19th century and has since become a symbol of the holiday season.

Christmas Cards Tradition:

The first commercial Christmas cards were commissioned in London in 1843. Sir Henry Cole, the director of the Victoria and Albert Museum, wanted a card he could send to friends and acquaintances to wish them a "Merry Christmas."

Santa Claus Evolution:

The modern image of Santa Claus is a result of various influences, including the Dutch figure Sinterklaas, British Father Christmas, and the 1823 poem "A Visit from St. Nicholas" (commonly known as "The Night Before Christmas").

Mistletoe Tradition:

The custom of kissing under the mistletoe has ancient roots. In Norse mythology, mistletoe was associated with love and friendship, and enemies who met under it were supposed to lay down their arms and embrace.
